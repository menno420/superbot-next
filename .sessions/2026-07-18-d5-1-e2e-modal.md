## Flow (grounded in the real corpus)

`!starboard panel` (staff) opens the real `starboard.config` panel → the panel's
`✏️ Threshold` action declares `THRESHOLD_MODAL` (`starboard.threshold_form`),
materialized by the REAL `sb/adapters/discord/modal_view.build_modal` into a real
`discord.ui.Modal` with the `threshold` TextInput → a real channel-pick component
click configures the hall-of-fame channel (default threshold 3) → `modal_submit`
with `threshold=7` re-enters through the frozen modal adapter (`dispatch_modal`)
→ the real `starboard.panel_threshold` handler runs the audited
`starboard.configure` K7 write (real Postgres) → `_reopen` re-renders
`starboard.config` through the real `build_embed`. Asserts BOTH the persisted
write (`service.get_settings` → threshold 7) AND the re-rendered embed
(`⭐ ≥ **7**`).

## ⟲ This-session review

The flow exercises the real modal band the way P1's fake transport cannot:
`modal_view.build_modal` materializes the real declared `ModalSpec` into a real
`discord.ui.Modal` + `TextInput`, and `dispatch_modal` re-enters the real
handler which runs an audited K7 write — asserted on BOTH the persisted row and
the re-rendered embed. One honest seam it does NOT close: the real modal-OPEN
still rides `build_modal` called directly rather than through a discord-shaped
responder's `open_modal` threaded from the button click (the harness's
`modal_submit` skips the issue leg, as the parity runner always has). Wiring a
recording responder that captures the real `open_modal(build_modal(...))` on the
✏️ Threshold click would fold the issue leg into the same recorder — the natural
convergence with the `DiscordPanelPresenter` dispatch-branch coverage #573/#574
both flagged.

## 💡 Idea

Extend `RealAdapterRecorder` with a `modals: list[ModalCapture]` sink by
re-pointing the responder's `open_modal` at the real `modal_view.build_modal`
(the presenter/emitter swap's twin) — so a modal ISSUE (button click → real
`discord.ui.Modal`) is captured end-to-end alongside the submit, and every future
`defer_mode=MODAL` action gets a one-line "the form materializes" e2e assertion
without hand-calling `build_modal`.
